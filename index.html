EA Visceral

During Summer 2016 and May to October 2017, I served as the UX/UI Design Intern for EA Visceral where I worked on an action-adventure Star Wars console game. I worked with engineers, artists, designers, and product managers to create motion mock ups and design fictional interfaces inspired by the Star Wars universe.

I worked alongside my mentor Justin Porter and Matthew Pearson as well as the gameplay and engineering teams to create cinematic UX , including user feedback systems, heads up displays for vehicles, and other diegetic communication features. I conceptualized, prototyped, and scripted game assets using Adobe CC and Frostbite - EA's in-house game engine.​​​​​​​

EA Maxis

From October to December 2017, I interned with the UX design team on Project Campfire at EA Maxis, working on the Sims Mobile app - the mobile adaptation from the popular franchise. I worked with designers, product managers, artists, and engineers to create a social networking feature on a game that has accumulated over 2 million downloads worldwide. The game is currently available for free download for Android and iOS devices.

Collegebacker

CollegeBacker is an online college savings account advisory service that helps families save money for college by helping users create a 529 savings account. The service is intended for millennial families but CollegeBacker also partners with institutions - schools, companies, and organizations - to integrate their service as part of educational matching.

I joined CollegeBacker in September to December as a part-time UX designer, working directly with founders Abby Chao and Jordan Lee.

Tesla

From January to August 2018, I interned as a UX design intern on the Supply Chain Automation team at Tesla. I worked with product managers and engineers to tackle tough technical challenges and create innovative new solutions to support the entirety of Tesla's supply chain.

Working as the only designer on the team was rewarding but a challenge. I conducted user testing and field research, created wireframes and prototypes, and constantly iterated on my work. I learned to pitch to key stakeholders, negotiate and compromise with differing opinions, and observed the inner workings of supply chain, manufacturing, and operations in one of the most innovative automotive companies in the world.

Orai

In January 2019, I joined a mobile speech coach startup called Orai as a product designer, growing their team to 18. I wore many different hats, conducting user research and testing, owning new features, iterating on existing ones, and assisted in creating marketing materials. I was laid off in June 2019.
