overview

From January 2018 to August 2018, I interned as a member of the Supply Chain Automation team at Tesla. Even as an intern, I served as the only designer on a group of two dozen engineers, product/project managers, and data scientists. The Supply Chain Automation team was unusual insofar that the team was highly experimental and unique to Tesla. I had the opportunity to develop software solutions, design semi-automated experiences, and collaborate across a variety of teams and branches in the company.

what I did

While I'm restricted by NDA from publishing any of the work I completed during my internship, I can provide a high-level overview of some of the projects I contributed to in the context of the supply chain:

sourcing

The first step in the supply chain is sourcing the raw materials and third-party suppliers for the vehicles. For this step, I designed a web-based tool that allowed users to track price history and compare supplier rates.


production & assembly

At this step, materials are collected and transformed into the various parts that will be assembled. One of the major projects I was involved with entailed overhauling the entire workflow of a design engineer's process. I conducted extensive user research - often meeting with direct users on site - to better understand how to improve the workflow and introduce a more efficient, accurate, and scalable software solution. The final product was a web based app with multiple features and semi-automated processes that vastly improved efficiency and reduced human error.


storage

The logistical challenge of tracking thousands of different parts across multiple products and locations posed an opportunity for my team to explore various concepts to optimize efficiency and accuracy. I worked on involved creating the interaction model for a suite of tablet apps that would communicate key data to one web interface in real-time, tracking thousands of shipments and interactions.


delivery & troubleshooting

Tesla is a machine that never stops, so in order to ensure processes were running as smoothly as possible it was important that we were able to resolve errors as quickly. On the supply chain, that meant ensuring users were able to report errors in any phase - whether in distribution or delivery - intuitively and quickly. I contributed to this important initiative by implementing a troubleshooting app for warehouse associates to automatically report damaged inventory and a scalable tracking map that allowed users to track the exact location and status of a vehicle as it left the factory to its intended destination.​​​​​​​
