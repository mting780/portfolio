overview

EA Visceral was a video game studio under the publisher Electronic Arts (EA). They specialized in third-person AAA games. In the summer of 2016 and from May-October of 2017, I interned as a UX/UI Design intern on their UX team. Throughout my time there, I worked on an undisclosed Star Wars game for console devices. In October 2017, EA shut down the studio and I was relocated to their Maxis studio. The UX team at Visceral consisted of two full-time motion graphics artists/designers, four engineers, a QA engineer, and a product manager. Throughout my time there, I worked most closely with the two other designers to create motion mock ups, and UI.

fictional UI

These screens were mock-ups for a new spaceship that was hijacked by the game protagonists from Imperial forces. As such, I looked to elements from the Star Wars Empire for inspiration for the interface elements and design.

user feedback indicators

One of the primary features I worked on was the design for enemy awareness meters. These were in-game UI elements that informed players how suspicious or "aware" the AI were of their presence during stealth play modes and are an integral part in any action-adventure game with AI.

Motion mock-ups allowed me to present UI in the context of the game and visualize how a user would encounter it in its various states.
